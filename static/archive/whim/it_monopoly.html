No, I think some perspective is needed here. First of all, if we think about everything that happens in the world, with Boko Haram etc. the word 'evil' isn't really appropriate to describe what Google is doing. Google has never been 'good' and they've never been 'evil', they're a corporation, like any other, and their remit as such is to serve their shareholders, and that really is it. They have no other responsibilities other than to obey the law, or at least not break the law too egregiously. Google is an ad company, and as such, whether we like it or not, part of being an ad company is that *we* are the product, not any of their software items. Google provides us with services and software we use to further their ad business. The benefits of that to us, are significant, we get a load of stuff for free, or very cheap. The software Google make is a symptom, not the cause, ads are the cause. The downside is that we are shown a lot of ads and privacy is invaded in pretty minor ways. For example, if I go on AirBNB, I'm suddenly getting a lot of ads for AirBNB. Same for a used car site I frequent. It's all pretty harmless really. Sure, they scan your email for information, but with all the hundreds of millions of users they have, it's automatic, it's not like some seedy little bloke is reading our emails for cheap kicks. Frankly, we're numbers to them, our details are hidden in plain sight, and I can't think of any genuine way in which my real privacy has been invaded. That said, Google is doing a lot of damage in computing too, same as all the other companies giving stuff away and making money on ads, it's a popularity and numbers game, it's nothing to do with quality, and now quality has to compete with free stuff, which is not a good place to be. Also, with the free stuff, you don't need 10,000 users to make money, you need 10,000,000, at least. That's squeezing out niche and alternative players because there is no money in selling ads to 10,000 people, but back in the old days, there certainly was money in selling a $50 software product to 10,000 people. Evil? No. Good? No. Google isn't there to be either, they are there to make money.

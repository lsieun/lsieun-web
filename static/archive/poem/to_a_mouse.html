Intro

"To a Mouse, on Turning Her Up in Her Nest With the Plough, November, 1785" is a Scots-language poem written by Robert Burns in 1785. According to legend, Burns was ploughing in the fields and accidentally destroyed a mouse's nest, which it needed to survive the winter. In fact, Burns's brother claimed that the poet composed the poem while still holding his plough.

“最好的计划，不管人和鼠，往往都会落空。” 这是诗人罗伯特·彭斯（Robert Burns，1759—1796）诗歌《致老鼠》（1785年，26岁）中的一句。他写这首诗，是因为有一天在犁地的时候不小心弄翻了老鼠窝，自己非常愧疚，于是慨叹人生也像这老鼠窝一般，总会被不可控的偶然所操纵，造化弄人。

Little, cunning, cowering, timorous beast,

I'm truly sorry man's dominion
Has broken Nature's social union,
And justifies that ill opinion
Which makes you startle
At me, your poor, earth-born companion
And fellow mortal!

What then? Poor beast, you must live!
An odd ear in twenty-four sheaves
Is a small request;
I will get a blessing with what is left,
And never miss it.

Your small house, too, in ruin!
Its feeble walls the winds are scattering!
And nothing now, to build a new one,
Of coarse green foliage!
And bleak December's winds ensuing,
Both bitter and piercing!

John Steinbeck took the title of his 1937 novel Of Mice and Men from a line contained in the penultimate倒数第二的 immediately before the last one stanza（诗的）节，段 a group of lines in a repeated pattern that form a unit in some types of poem: "The best laid schemes o' mice an' men / Gang aft agley" (often paraphrased in English as "The best-laid plans of mice and men / Go oft awry").

在彭斯（Robert Burns）这首诗歌问世150多年以后，它感动了一位美国的工人作家，那就是《愤怒的葡萄》的作者约翰·斯坦贝克（John Steinbeck）。他受到「最好的计划，不管人和鼠，往往都会落空」这句话的影响，写出了中篇小说的典范之作《人鼠之间》。 斯坦贝克希望通过小说来回应彭斯的这首诗。在他看来，大萧条时代的工人，生活在鼠与人之间，他们的生活也受着命运无常的捉弄。
